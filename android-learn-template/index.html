One of the first steps towards achieving your financial goals is to create a budget that you can stick to, month after month.

The goal is to be cash flow positive and have money left over after your expenses and debts are paid to start saving or investing.

Step-by-step budget guide

1. Gather together all your financial statements – bank accounts, credit card bills, investments, recent utility bills and any information about sources of income or expense.

The more information you have, the easier it will be to create a monthly average.

2. Record all sources of income - salary (net income, or take home pay) and any outside income (for example, interest on investments).

Record your total income as a monthly amount.

3. List all monthly expenses – mortgage repayment, car repayment, insurance, groceries, petrol, train/bus travel, utilities, entertainment, school fees, etc.

4. Split expenses into fixed and variable - fixed expenses broadly stay the same each month (such as mortgage/rent, insurance or car loan).

Variable expenses change from month to month (such as groceries, petrol and entertainment).

5. Total income and expenses - if your income exceeds your expenses, you’re off to a good start. If it’s the other way round, you’ll need to make some changes.

6. Make adjustments to expenses - if you have accurately identified and listed all expenses, the goal is to balance your income and expense columns.

This means all income is accounted for and budgeted to a specific expense, including contributions to a savings account. If your expenses exceed your income, look at your variable expenses to see what can be cut.

7. Review regularly - every month, compare your actual expenses with your budgeted ones. This will show you where you’re doing well and where you may need to improve.

Strategies to reduce your debt

  • Pay off non-deductible debt first
  • Don't borrow for depreciating assets
  • Pay off high interest credit cards
  • Always plan for the unexpected
