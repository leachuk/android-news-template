First home buyers in New South Wales will benefit from 1 July 2014 with the First Home Owners Grant (FHOG) threshold increasing to $750,000, the state government announced today.

The $15,000 grant is currently available on property purchases up to the value of $650,000.

The NSW Budget Papers revealed that the number of FHOGs for new homes grew rapidly over 2013-14. In the first four months of 2014 the number of grants paid for new homes was 46% higher than in the corresponding period of 2013.

NSW Treasury figures show that targeting homebuyer assistance towards new homes has had a positive impact on housing supply.

In the first four months of 2014, the number of private sector residential building approvals in NSW was 24% higher compared with the corresponding period of the previous year.

The government believes that increasing housing supply should help to slow house price growth and have a positive impact on housing affordability.

First Home Owner Grants in NSW

Eligibility for the $5,000 New Home Owners Grant (NHOG) will now be restricted to Australian citizens and permanent residents from 1 July 2014.

The scheme was introduced to stimulate the construction of new dwellings and help those eligible to purchase new homes, homes off the plan and vacant land on which a new home will be built.
